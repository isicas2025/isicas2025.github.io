Paper Submission

Instructions for Authors

As the ISICAS symposium will not produce proceedings, all the accepted papers will be published in four Special Issues of the IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems I: Regular Papers (TCAS-I), of the IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems II: Express Briefs (TCAS-II), of the IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Circuits and Systems (TBioCAS), of the IEEE Transactions on Very Large Scale Integration Systems (TVLSI), of the IEEE Open Journal of Circuits and Systems (OJCAS), and of the IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Artificial Intelligence (TCASAI). The  IEEE ISICAS 2025  paper submission and review process will follow the standard procedure of the journals' special issue paper submissions.


Reminder

At least one author per paper must be registered AND attend the conference to present the paper (Oral and Poster). In case of a no-show, the paper will be excluded from the special issue and will not be published. Please note that, in addition to the general scope requirement of TCAS-I/-II/TBioCAS/TVLSI/OJCAS/TCASAI, for the  ISICAS special issue, manuscripts on analog and mixed-signal, nonlinear, communications, power, and biomedical circuits must have an on-chip implementation and measured silicon results. Manuscripts on digital circuits and systems and VLSI must have at least FPGA verification. Submissions not compliant with the above requirements will not be reviewed and will be returned to the authors. Extended versions of recently published conference papers with significant additional materials are also welcome.

Paper Preparation and Submission

Prospective authors are invited to submit full-length papers with a complete description of their integrated circuits and systems implementations and collected measurement results. Extended versions of previously published recent conference papers with significant additional materials (conforming to the rules of TCAS-I, TCAS-II, TBioCAS, TVLSI, OJCAS, and TCASAI see below) are also welcomed.

Manuscripts must follow the standard single-spaced, double-column IEEE Transactions format, as specified at the following link: http://ieeeauthorcenter.ieee.org/create-your-ieee-article/use-authoring-tools-and-ieee-article-templates/ieee-article-templates/templates-for-transactions/

Requirement for IEEE TVLSI Submission: 

TVLSI emphasizes system-level innovations or implications across system, chip, and package levels, as well as HW/SW integration. It focuses on large-scale systems-on-chip (SoCs), FPGA-based designs, 2.5D/3D chiplet-based systems-in-package, system-on-interposer, or multi-die integrations, including die-to-die interfaces. Contributions should address holistic platform design challenges beyond single IC components.
